About the Workshop
Health technology assessment is rapidly emerging as a cornerstone of evidence-based health policymaking in India, with the Department of Health Research establishing Regional Resource Centres across the country to strengthen the capacity for conducting rigorous cost-effectiveness analyses. R, an open-source programming language, has become the most widely used tool for HTA modelling globally, offering transparency, reproducibility, and a rich ecosystem of packages purpose-built for health economic evaluation. This three-day hands-on workshop, organised by RRC-HTA, AIIMS Bhopal, is designed to enable HTA scientists to transition from spreadsheet-based analyses to R — building four complete models using clinical case studies (e.g. GDM screening, coronary stent selection, CKD progression, and trastuzumab for breast cancer), each accompanied by ready-to-use code templates and interactive Shiny applications. No prior R programming experience is required.
